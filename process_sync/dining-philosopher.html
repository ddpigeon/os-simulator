Dining Philosopher Problem

The Dining Philosophers problem is a synchronization challenge in which a group of philosophers sit around a dining table with forks between them. Each philosopher needs two forks to eat, and the problem arises when all philosophers attempt to pick up the forks simultaneously, leading to a deadlock. Techniques like semaphores or mutexes are used to manage access to the forks and prevent deadlocks, ensuring that philosophers can eat without causing conflicts.
